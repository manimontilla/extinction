The Baiji

China's Blue Dolphin

Meet the Baiji

Also known as the China's blue dolphin, the Baiji used to swim freely in the Yangtze River.

The Baiji has been a ptotected species since 1975. Now the Baiji is functionally extinct.

In 2006, scientists from the Baiji Foundation travelled up the Yantze River for more than 2,000 miles in search of any surviving specimen, but they were unable to find any.

This means that are too little breeding pairs remained to ensure the species's survival.

What happened?

It was your fault!!

Not you specifically, but the Baiji are considered the first Dolphins to become extinct due to humans.

Harmful fishing practices led to the deterioration of their environment which means no more little dolphins.
